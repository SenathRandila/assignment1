Protected & Conserved Areas

Overview

Protected and Conserved Areas (PCAs), which encompass Indigenous and Community Conserved Areas (ICCAs), stand out as highly effective
tools in preventing the decline of natural ecosystems and species. They play a crucial role in achieving long-term sustainable development,
aligning with targets like Aichi 11 and 12, as well as several Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). In certain regions, PCAs serve as focal
points for economic development, fostering tourism, sustainable resource utilization, and acting as vital sources of freshwater. Furthermore,
PCAs contribute significantly to food security by preserving ecosystem services that support agriculture, safeguarding resources essential for
crop breeding programs, and providing space for traditional biodiversity-friendly farming and grazing systems. Additionally, PCAs play a major
role in enhancing climate resilience, actively storing and sequestering carbon, and ensuring that ecosystems continue to provide essential goods
and services to human societies.
